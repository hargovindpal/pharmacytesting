SCOPE AFTER D.PHARM

HOSPITAL PHARMACIST :

Pharmacists have a crucial role to play in both government and private hospitals, managing the procurement and supply of drugs and inventory. They also take care of catering to medicine requirements based on prescriptions and the needs of doctors.

CLINICAL PHARMACIST :

Diploma in pharmacy holders have various roles to play in clinics as well, which are similar to those in hospitals. Clinical pharmacists work directly with physicians, other health professionals, and patients to ensure that the medications prescribed for patients contribute to the best possible health outcomes.

RETAIL PHARMACIST :

Retail pharmacists (also known as community pharmacists) are responsible for dispensing and controlling both prescription and non-prescription medicine. They must work to legal and ethical guidelines to ensure that medication is kept secure and given to the correct patients in the right quantities.

WHOLESALE PHARMACIST :

A Wholesale Pharmacist is a licensed healthcare professional responsible for managing and overseeing the pharmaceutical distribution and supply chain operations within a wholesale or Distribution Company. Wholesale Pharmacists collaborates with manufacturers, suppliers, healthcare institutions, and retail pharmacies to ensure the safe and timely delivery of pharmaceutical products.

MEDICALREPRESENTATIVE :

A medical representative promotes and sells pharmaceutical products to healthcare specialists, such as doctors and pharmacists. They provide information about various products' benefits, usage and dosage. They also help to build professional relationships with practitioners to increase product awareness and sales.

PHARMACY DATA ANALYST :

The primary focus of the Pharmacy Data Analyst is to support the operational efficiency of the Pharmacy Department by coordinating fiscal management activities, performing data analysis, and maintaining departmental statistics and reports.

PATHALOGICAL LAB SCIENTIST :

Pathological Lab Scientist utilizes lab equipment to analyze different samples and specimens that usually come from human subjects to find the abnormalities and understand the cause.
